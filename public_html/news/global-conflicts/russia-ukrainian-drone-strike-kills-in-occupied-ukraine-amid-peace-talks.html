Russia: Ukrainian drone strike kills 24 in occupied Ukraine amid peace talks


Russian authorities reported on January 1, 2026, that a Ukrainian drone attack resulted in the deaths of at least 24 individuals and injuries to more than 50 others during New Year's celebrations in the Russian-occupied village of Khorly, located in Ukraine's Kherson region.

This incident occurred amid escalating bilateral tensions, notwithstanding recent diplomatic assessments characterizing peace negotiations as productive.

According to Vladimir Saldo, the Moscow-appointed governor of the occupied Kherson region, three unmanned aerial vehicles targeted a café and hotel in the Black Sea coastal resort town, with one reportedly deploying an incendiary device that ignited a fire. Ukrainian authorities have not issued an immediate response to these allegations, and independent verification of the event remains unavailable.The incident drew condemnation from several Russian officials.

Valentina Matviyenko, Speaker of the Federation Council (Russia's upper parliamentary chamber), asserted that the attack reinforced Moscow's determination to expeditiously attain its objectives in the nearly four-year conflict, adding that it reaffirmed the legitimacy of Russia's original demands.This development follows Moscow's prior accusations that Ukraine conducted a long-range drone assault on one of President Vladimir Putin's official residences in northwestern Russia earlier in the week, claims which Kyiv has dismissed as fabricated.

Russia's Ministry of Defense stated that experts had retrieved navigational data from a recovered drone, purportedly confirming the residence as the intended target. No supporting evidence was publicly disclosed, though officials indicated that the information would be shared with U.S. counterparts via established diplomatic channels.Additionally, the ministry released video footage depicting a damaged drone in a snowy area, presented as evidence related to the alleged assault.



The recording, featuring an unidentified individual in military attire discussing the wreckage, lacked contextual details such as location or timestamp, rendering independent corroboration impossible.Ukrainian officials have characterized the allegations concerning Putin's residence as a pretext designed to undermine advancing peace talks, which have intensified transatlantically in recent weeks.

In his New Year's address, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy indicated that a potential peace agreement was approximately 90% complete, while cautioning that the unresolved 10%—likely encompassing critical issues such as territorial disputes—would profoundly influence the prospects for enduring peace in Ukraine and Europe.

U.S. Special Envoy Steve Witkoff reported a productive discussion involving himself, Secretary of State Marco Rubio, and presidential advisor Jared Kushner with national security advisors from the United Kingdom, France, Germany, and Ukraine. The dialogue centered on advancing practical measures within the peace framework, including enhanced security assurances and deconfliction protocols to terminate hostilities and prevent recurrence.

Ukrainian lead negotiator Rustem Umerov confirmed forthcoming meetings between European and Ukrainian officials, with President Zelenskyy scheduled for consultations with European leaders in the near term.In related diplomatic efforts, Kyiv has urged the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) to address alleged Russian assaults on Ukrainian electrical infrastructure vital to nuclear safety.

The IAEA disseminated a Ukrainian diplomatic note detailing power disruptions at multiple nuclear facilities stemming from reported Russian drone and missile strikes on December 23.Separately, Russian forces conducted overnight drone operations targeting civilian infrastructure in Ukraine's Odesa region, as reported by regional governor Oleh Kiper.

Damage was sustained to a multi-story residential structure and a high-rise apartment, though no casualties were recorded.Ukraine's air force reported that defenses neutralized or disrupted 176 of 205 incoming drones nationwide, with impacts from 24 drones noted at 15 sites; operations were described as ongoing at the time of reporting.
